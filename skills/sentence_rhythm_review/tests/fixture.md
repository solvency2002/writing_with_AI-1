Screening the literature is slow work, and the burden falls hardest on the
first pass. Reviewers read many records to find a few. Tools now promise to
lighten that pass, but their behavior varies from one review to another. The
question is not whether they are accurate on average but where they diverge.

The model reads a record. It applies the criteria. It returns a verdict.
Sometimes the verdict is right. Sometimes it is wrong. The reviewer checks
each case. The pattern only shows up in aggregate. No single record tells the
whole story.

The most important caveat is a subtle one, because a tool that scores well on
overall accuracy can still discard the handful of records that would have
changed the review's conclusion, and that failure hides completely behind a
reassuring headline number. Explicit criteria help. They cannot remove every
ambiguous case.
