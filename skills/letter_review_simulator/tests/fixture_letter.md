Dear Editor,

The authors are to be commended for attempting a mortality analysis, even if such retrospective work is rarely informative. Their effort to look at steroid timing is at least a starting point for those without access to trial data.

The authors claim that corticosteroids cure sepsis when given early. This conclusion ignores confounding by indication, because sicker patients are managed differently from the outset. A retrospective cohort cannot establish that steroids eliminate mortality.

The authors also use causal language for an observational design. In reality, their early-steroid protocol caused the lower mortality only because the healthier patients were selected into that group. The timing recommendation is therefore unsupported.

We urge the authors to temper their claims.

Sincerely,
A. Reviewer
