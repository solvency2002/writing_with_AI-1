Medicine. "Early corticosteroid therapy and mortality in adults with sepsis:
a single-center retrospective cohort."

**Design (as stated):** single-center retrospective cohort study.

**Objective (verbatim):** "To evaluate whether the timing of corticosteroid
administration is associated with in-hospital mortality in adults with
sepsis."

**Methods (summary):** 612 adults with sepsis over 4 years. "Early" therapy
defined as corticosteroids within 6 hours of sepsis recognition (n = 214)
versus no early therapy (n = 398). Primary outcome: in-hospital mortality.
Adjusted for age, sex, and Charlson comorbidity index.

**Results (verbatim):** "In-hospital mortality was 18.2% in the early-therapy
group and 27.1% in the no-early-therapy group (adjusted odds ratio 0.71, 95%
CI 0.52–0.97). In the septic shock subgroup (n = 96), mortality was 31% versus
40% (p = 0.21)."

**Conclusion (verbatim):** "Early corticosteroids reduce mortality in sepsis
and should be initiated within 6 hours of recognition. Patients with septic
shock particularly benefited."
