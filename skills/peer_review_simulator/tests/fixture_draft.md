# Abstract

We report an 80-year-old man who developed acute eosinophilic pneumonia after daptomycin treatment for a deep-seated soft tissue infection. Daptomycin caused the eosinophilic pneumonia, which resolved completely with corticosteroid therapy.

# Introduction

Daptomycin is a cyclic lipopeptide antibiotic used for gram-positive infections. Adverse pulmonary reactions are uncommon but have been reported.

# Case Presentation

An 80-year-old man with a deep-seated soft tissue infection received intravenous daptomycin starting on Day 0. On Day 12 he developed dyspnea and fever. Chest CT showed bilateral peripheral consolidation. Bronchoalveolar lavage demonstrated 42% eosinophils. Daptomycin was discontinued and oral prednisolone was initiated at 0.5 mg/kg/day on Day 13. Symptoms resolved completely by Day 23.

# Discussion

This case demonstrates that daptomycin caused acute eosinophilic pneumonia in our patient. The temporal relationship between drug exposure and symptom onset, together with the BAL eosinophilia, proves the causal role of daptomycin. All previous reports show identical clinical features, and our case adds further confirmation that daptomycin must always be discontinued immediately when respiratory symptoms develop during therapy. Corticosteroids accelerate recovery and should be considered standard treatment.

# Conclusion

Clinicians should be aware that daptomycin causes acute eosinophilic pneumonia. Prompt discontinuation and corticosteroids are mandatory.
