## Article types

This journal publishes Case Reports, Letters to the Editor, and Brief
Reviews. The rules below apply to **Case Reports** only; other article types
have separate instructions on a different page.

## Title

- The title must contain the phrase "case report" (any capitalization).
- Title length: up to 150 characters including spaces.

## Abstract

- Unstructured abstract.
- Abstract must not exceed 250 words.

## Body sections (required, in this order)

1. Introduction
2. Case Presentation
3. Discussion
4. Conclusion

References go after Conclusion. Acknowledgements, Funding, Competing
Interests, and Patient Consent statements go between References and the end
of the manuscript.

## Figures and tables

- Maximum 5 figures + tables combined.
- Figures: 300 dpi minimum, PNG or TIFF.
- Each figure must have a caption beneath it.

## References and citation style

- Vancouver style. Submit references in the format produced by the journal's
  CSL file `vancouver.csl` (available from the journal's resource page).
- Up to 30 references for case reports.

## Patient consent

- Written informed consent must be obtained from the patient (or the
  patient's legal guardian / next of kin) for publication of the case
  report and any accompanying images.
- A statement to this effect must appear in the manuscript, in the Patient
  Consent section after References.

## Ethics

- IRB approval is not required for single-patient case reports at most
  institutions, but if your institution requires it, state the approval
  number in an Ethics section.

## Author declarations

- Author contributions, Competing Interests, and Funding statements are
  mandatory. Acknowledgements are optional.

## Keywords

- Provide 3 to 6 keywords. MeSH terms are preferred but not required.

## Generative-AI disclosure

- If any generative-AI tool (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) contributed to
  manuscript writing, editing, or figure generation, this must be disclosed
  in a dedicated "AI Disclosure" subsection at the end of the manuscript,
  with the tool name, version, and the scope of its use.

## Data availability

- Not applicable to case reports (a data-availability statement is **not
  required**).
