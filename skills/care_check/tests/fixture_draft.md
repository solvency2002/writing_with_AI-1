# Late presentation of myocarditis

## Abstract

A previously healthy patient presented with chest pain and was eventually
diagnosed with myocarditis. Treatment was supportive. The patient recovered.

## Introduction

Myocarditis in young adults can be missed because the presentation overlaps
with benign chest-pain syndromes. We describe one such case.

## Patient information

The patient was a young adult who presented with three days of pleuritic
chest pain and low-grade fever. There was no relevant past medical history
mentioned by the patient.

## Clinical findings

On examination the patient appeared unwell. Cardiac auscultation revealed
a soft S3.

## Diagnostic assessment

Troponin was elevated. Cardiac MRI was consistent with myocarditis.

## Therapeutic intervention

The patient was treated with bisoprolol and an ACE inhibitor. Symptoms
improved.

## Follow-up

LVEF improved from 38% on admission to 55% at three months. No further
hospitalisations occurred.

## Discussion

This case underlines the difficulty of recognising myocarditis at first
presentation. Limitations include the single-patient observation.

The take-away is that troponin should be measured liberally in young adults
with new pleuritic chest pain [@ref1].
