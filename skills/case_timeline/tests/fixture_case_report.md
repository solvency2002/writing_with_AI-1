# Patient information

A woman in her mid-30s, gravida 2 para 2, with an uncomplicated vaginal
delivery 12 days before presentation. No prior cardiovascular history. She
was discharged home on Day -10. No hypertension, dyslipidemia, or diabetes.

# Clinical findings

On re-presentation to the emergency department on Day 1, blood pressure was
138/86 mmHg, heart rate 102/min, and SpO2 98 % on room air. Lung fields were
clear and cardiac auscultation revealed no murmurs or gallops.

# Diagnostic assessment

Serial 12-lead ECG, high-sensitivity troponin I at 0 and 3 hours,
transthoracic echocardiography, and invasive coronary angiography. The final
diagnosis was spontaneous coronary artery dissection, Saw type 2, mid left
anterior descending artery, with non-ST-elevation myocardial infarction.

# Therapeutic intervention

A conservative strategy without percutaneous intervention was chosen.
Aspirin 100 mg daily and clopidogrel 75 mg daily were started on Day 2.
Bisoprolol 2.5 mg daily and ramipril 2.5 mg daily were added before
discharge on Day 6. Ramipril was discontinued at Month 3 after blood
pressure normalized.

# Follow-up and outcomes

LVEF improved from 48 % at admission to 58 % at three months. No recurrent
ischemic events occurred through six months. The patient returned to
baseline activity tolerance by month 4.
